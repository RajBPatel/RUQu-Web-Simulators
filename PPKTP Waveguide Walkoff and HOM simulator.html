1. The Joint Spectral Amplitude (JSA)

The state of the photon pair generated via SPDC is defined by the Joint Spectral Amplitude, $F(\omega_s, \omega_i)$. It is the product of energy conservation (the pump envelope), momentum conservation (the phase-matching function), and the applied spectral filters:

$$F(\omega_s, \omega_i) = \alpha(\omega_s + \omega_i) \Phi(\omega_s, \omega_i) f_s(\omega_s) f_i(\omega_i)$$

  • $\alpha(\omega_s + \omega_i)$: The pump envelope (e.g., Gaussian, Sech$^2$, Top-hat), mapping the energy conservation $E_p = E_s + E_i$.
  • $\Phi(\omega_s, \omega_i)$: The phase-matching function, typically $\text{sinc}(\Delta k L / 2)$ for a crystal of length $L$.
  • $f(\omega)$: Spectral filters placed in the signal and idler arms.

2. Symmetric Group Velocity Matching (SGVM)

The phase mismatch parameter $\Delta k$ can be expanded to first order around the central frequencies:

$$\Delta k \approx (\omega_s - \omega_{s0}) D_{ps} + (\omega_i - \omega_{i0}) D_{pi}$$

Where $D_{\mu \nu} = \frac{1}{v_{g,\mu}} - \frac{1}{v_{g,\nu}}$ is the inverse group velocity mismatch (temporal walkoff). In standard crystals, this creates highly asymmetric, entangled states.

In Type-II ppKTP at exactly 775 nm $\rightarrow$ 1550 nm, a rare property occurs: the pump group velocity perfectly bisects the signal and idler group velocities ($v_{g,p} \approx \frac{v_{g,s} + v_{g,i}}{2}$). This makes $D_{ps} \approx -D_{pi}$.

This SGVM condition causes the phase-matching function to cross the pump function at precisely 90 degrees, resulting in a completely round, uncorrelated, and spectrally pure JSA main lobe without the need for narrow filtering.

3. Hong-Ou-Mandel (HOM) Interference

To strictly account for spectral asymmetry, non-degeneracy, and quantum beating without mathematical shortcuts, the simulator calculates the exact normalized 2D Fourier overlap integral between the JSA and its exchange-swapped self at every delay point $\tau$:

$$R_c(\tau) = 1 - \mathcal{D} \frac{\text{Re} \iint F(\omega_s, \omega_i) F^*(\omega_i, \omega_s) e^{-i (\omega_s - \omega_i) \tau} d\omega_s d\omega_i}{\iint |F(\omega_s, \omega_i)|^2 d\omega_s d\omega_i}$$

The term $e^{-i (\omega_s - \omega_i) \tau}$ translates the spectral information into the temporal wavepacket delay. If the photons are non-degenerate ($\omega_{s0} \neq \omega_{i0}$), this exponential term drives oscillatory quantum beating directly inside the HOM dip envelope.

4. Classical Degradations ($\mathcal{D}$)

Mismatches in the physical apparatus do not alter the intrinsic quantum state, but they introduce distinguishability that suppresses the interference probability. These are bundled into the scalar $\mathcal{D}$:

$$\mathcal{D} = \eta_{BS} \cdot \eta_{Pol} \cdot \eta_{Spatial}$$

  • Beam Splitter Imbalance: $\eta_{BS} = 4RT$, where $R$ and $T$ are the reflectivity and transmissivity.
  • Polarization Mismatch: $\eta_{Pol} = \cos^2(\Delta \theta)$, following Malus's Law.
  • Spatial Overlap: The analytical 2D overlap of two misaligned Gaussian beams defined by their $1/e^2$ waists ($w$) and lateral offsets ($\Delta x, \Delta y$):

    $$\eta_{Spatial} = \left( \frac{2 w_{x1} w_{x2}}{w_{x1}^2 + w_{x2}^2} \right) \exp\left( - \frac{2 \Delta x^2}{w_{x1}^2 + w_{x2}^2} \right) \times \left( \frac{2 w_{y1} w_{y2}}{w_{y1}^2 + w_{y2}^2} \right) \exp\left( - \frac{2 \Delta y^2}{w_{y1}^2 + w_{y2}^2} \right)$$
